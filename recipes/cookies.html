Cookies

A warm, soft & chewy Chocolate Chip Cookie is divine! These are the type of Chocolate Chip Cookies that I grew up eating and, to me, they are true comfort foods. The recipe that I use, I found on the bag of Trader Joe’s Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips; and, it is so good that I don’t have the need to look for a recipe. The Trader Joe’s recipe is simple and I have made my own modifications and details which allows me to bake perfect cookies on every batch!

Ingredients

  • 2 ¼ cups of flour
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 cup of brown sugar firmly packed
  • ½ cup of sugar
  • 2 sticks of butter room temperature
  • 1 ½ teaspoon of Vanilla Extract
  • 2 eggs room temperature
  • 1 pack of Semi-Sweet Chocolate Chips

Directions

  1. Add flour, salt, baking soda into a bowl and set aside.
  2. Add brown sugar, sugar, and butter into a bowl and beat with an electric mixer until light and fluffy. This takes me about 3 minutes.
  3. Add eggs and vanilla and mix again until the mixture is light and fluffy again.
  4. Slowly add the dry ingredients to the sugar/butter mixture. With a spoon slowly stir in the dry mixture until it is fully combined. I add a third of the mixture at a time until it is combined; and then, I do a quick mix of the batter with the electric mixer.
  5. Add the Chocolate Chips and mix until they are incorporated into the batter.
  6. Chill the batter for at least 3 hours.
  7. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
  8. Put a sheet of parchment paper or silicone baking mat on the baking sheet.
  9. Scoop batter into even balls of batter and place on the baking sheet. I use a spoon to scoop my batter, but you can use a batter scoop. Here is a good article about batter scoops and the size of cookies by the Bakers Brigade.
  10. Bake for 14 minutes.
  11. Take out of the oven, let cookies sit on the tray for one minute, and then cool the cookies on the cooling tray.
  12. Bake for 14 minutes. *Timing is determined by size of batter scoops, oven's cooking time, the temperature of batter.
